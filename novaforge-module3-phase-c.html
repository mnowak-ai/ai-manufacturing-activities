Safety Decision —
Recurring AI Alerts

🛡️Situation · Assembly Line B & Robotics Station 4

The NovaForge AI safety monitoring system has generated 12 alerts this week — a mix of critical motor temperature warnings on Arm-02, recurring encoder faults on Arm-04 every night, and three proximity alerts at Robotics Station 4 that fired when no workers were present.

Technicians have been manually resetting equipment after alerts and continuing operations. No injuries have occurred — but two critical thermal shutdowns happened this week and one encoder failure took Arm-04 fully offline.

Operational context — what your investigation found
  • Arm-02 motor temperature escalated from 71°C to 91°C across the week — a predictable progression toward catastrophic failure
  • Arm-04 encoder faults follow a night-shift-only pattern — the encoder cable has been flagged as worn since February and never replaced
  • Station 4 proximity alerts show AI confidence of 29–38% — consistently the lowest in the log, suggesting sensor miscalibration
  • Maintenance requests for Arm-02 and Arm-04 were submitted and not actioned for 4+ days
  • Technician M. Reyes formally documented that a failure was preventable — creating a liability record if ignored
AI Safety System Recommendation
"Alert pattern analysis suggests Sensor SN-3T miscalibration as the primary driver of elevated alert frequency. Recommend sensor replacement and recalibration of Station 4 proximity sensor. Arm-02 and Arm-04 thermal/encoder patterns are within recoverable variance — continue monitoring."
⚠ AI recommendation based on alert frequency and deviation data. Does not account for escalating thermal progression, February cable flag, or documented maintenance failures.
Your decision
Full Safety Shutdown. You've prioritized worker safety over production output. Justify what specific conditions would need to be met before production resumes — and how you'd communicate the shutdown to leadership and clients.
Targeted Inspection. Balanced approach — addressing the documented mechanical risks without a full shutdown. Explain exactly what gets fixed, by when, and what monitoring thresholds would trigger escalation to a full shutdown.
Continue Operations. You're following the AI recommendation. Justify how you would address the documented Arm-02 thermal escalation and the February cable issue — both of which the AI recommendation does not resolve.

Compliance Decision —
Audit Status Review

📋Situation · Compliance System Status

The NovaForge Compliance Tracking System is showing a "mostly compliant" overall status — 5 items green, the rest flagged. The system automated dashboard suggests the facility is within an acceptable compliance range for the upcoming audit.

However, your Phase A and Phase B investigation painted a different picture. The compliance gaps are not just administrative delays — they are documented, systemic, and in some cases directly contributing to safety and quality failures this week.

What the compliance data actually shows
  • Arm-02 motor service is 27 days overdue — a direct, documented contributor to this week's critical thermal shutdowns
  • Conveyor-01 belt replacement was requested 3 weeks ago — parts never ordered, leading to the 155-minute line-down event Friday
  • Station 4 proximity sensor calibration is 9 days overdue — directly explaining the three false positive safety alerts this week
  • QC inspection camera calibration is 4 days delayed — contributing to the AI camera missing defects caught manually by J. Park
  • Supervisor safety sign-offs were missed on 3 shifts this week — a recurring pattern the Safety Officer formally documented
  • Technician S. Huang documented the Arm-04 cable issue four times since February — no action taken
AI Compliance System Output
"Compliance score: 72/100. Facility meets minimum operational compliance threshold. 5 of 14 tracked items show overdue status. Administrative delays account for most gaps. Recommend scheduling maintenance clearance within the next 30-day window before audit date."
⚠ This score reflects completion rates only — it does not weight the operational consequences of specific gaps or account for the systemic nature of maintenance delays.
Your decision
Readiness approved. You're accepting the system score. Justify why you believe a 72/100 score — with Arm-02, Conveyor-01, and Station 4 overdue — is sufficient for an external audit, given the documented consequences of those specific gaps.
Flagged for correction. Targeted remediation approach. Name the specific items you are requiring to be resolved, the deadlines, and who is accountable — before the audit team arrives.
Full reset required. You're stopping audit preparation until the systemic issues are resolved. Explain what 'full compliance reset' means in practice — and how long you believe it will take to get there.

Readiness Decision

This is the final operational decision of the mission. Based on everything your investigation found — safety, compliance, quality, AI analysis, and human judgment — you must determine NovaForge's audit readiness.

What this decision means

An external audit team will review NovaForge's safety records, maintenance logs, compliance documentation, and quality control results. A "Ready" verdict invites that review now. A "Not Ready" verdict delays the audit — with operational and contractual implications. A "Ready with Conditions" means the audit proceeds, but with specific items flagged as remediation requirements.

Whatever you decide, you must be able to defend it with evidence from your investigation.
