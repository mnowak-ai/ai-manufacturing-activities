Section 01

Mission Briefing — What Is Happening?

Something is wrong on the NovaBot production line. Over the past two weeks, the NovaForge operations team has recorded a growing number of incidents — and nobody has a clear answer yet about what is causing them.

🦾 Robotic Arm Errors
Arms R-4 and R-7 are stopping mid-cycle multiple times per shift. The error codes change each time — no consistent pattern found yet.
⏱ Unexpected Downtime
Three machines went offline without warning this week. Unplanned downtime is up 40 minutes per shift compared to last month.
📡 Sensor Irregularities
Vibration and temperature sensors at stations 3 and 5 are giving readings that don't match manual checks. Something is off.
📦 Production Delays
Output is down 23% from last month. Two shipment deadlines have already been missed. A third is at risk this week.

Leadership needs answers — fast

NovaForge leadership does not yet know the root cause. They have assigned your team to investigate the situation before it gets worse. Your job in Phase A is to review the evidence and build a picture of what is actually happening.

Section 02

Your Role as an Investigator

In Phase A, you are not using AI tools yet. You are doing the foundational work that makes AI analysis useful — reviewing the actual evidence and thinking critically about what it means.

🔍 Operations Investigator
📊 Troubleshooting Analyst
🔧 Manufacturing Support Specialist

The golden rule of troubleshooting

Good troubleshooting starts with understanding the data yourself — before handing it to any tool or system. If you don't understand the evidence first, you won't be able to evaluate whether the AI's analysis is correct. Your job right now: read, observe, and think.

You will collect and organize evidence from five sources inside the NovaForge investigation packet. Then you will identify patterns, form hypotheses, and document your findings so they are ready for AI analysis in Phase B.

Section 04

Task 1 — Review the Evidence

You've reviewed the five data sources. Now it's time to examine the data more deliberately. Work through the three steps below.

  1. 1
    Scan all five data sources — go back through each tab and make mental notes about anything that stands out. Don't skip the technician notes — real-world observations often contain details that logs miss.
  2. 2
    Look for patterns — are the same machines failing repeatedly? Do issues happen more on certain shifts? Are any machines overdue for maintenance AND showing errors? Do sensor readings match what technicians are observing?
  3. 3
    Flag anything unusual or inconsistent — pay special attention to situations where two or more data sources point to the same problem, or where data sources contradict each other.

Quick Reflection Checkpoint

Before you complete your report, answer these short reflection questions. Keep your answers practical and direct — one to three sentences each.

Prompt 01
What patterns stood out most in the data?
Think about which findings showed up across multiple data sources.
Prompt 02
Which machine appears to fail most often — and why do you think that is?
Look at error frequency, maintenance history, and technician notes together.
Prompt 03
What types of issues seem most common — mechanical, sensor, maintenance, or operational?
You may find more than one category is involved.
Prompt 04
What additional information would help confirm your hypotheses?

Prompt 05
Did the technician notes reveal anything that the logs alone didn't show?

Prompt 06
If you had to act on one finding right now — before AI analysis — what would you do first?

Section 08

Prepare for AI Analysis

Your human investigation is complete

In Phase B, you will take the evidence you've gathered here and run it through AI tools. The AI will help you look for patterns you may have missed, generate additional troubleshooting hypotheses, and organize your findings into a structured diagnostic report.

In Phase B, AI tools will help you:

  • ›Analyze your investigation data and identify patterns across all five data sources simultaneously
  • ›Surface connections and patterns that are harder to spot manually across large datasets
  • ›Generate structured troubleshooting insights and potential root cause explanations
  • ›Compare AI findings with your human observations — and identify where they agree or differ

Critical reminder before Phase B

AI will help you analyze the data — but it cannot replace your judgment. You already know context the AI doesn't have: what the technicians said, what the floor looks like, what felt off when you reviewed the logs. Your job in Phase B is to use AI as a tool, then validate its output against your own investigation.
