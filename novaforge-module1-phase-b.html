Phase B · Screen 6 of 10 — Critical Thinking

When AI Gets
It Wrong

AI tools can be confidently wrong. This is one of the most important things for operations professionals to understand. Read the scenario below carefully.

AI Recommendation Alert

Scenario: The Overnight Staffing Cut

The NovaForge operations AI analyzed production data for the past 90 days. It found that overnight shifts (10pm–6am) produce 18% fewer units than day shifts. The AI's recommendation:

"Reduce overnight staffing by 40% to improve cost efficiency. Data shows overnight production is consistently lower and can be maintained with fewer workers."

This recommendation looks logical at first. The data is real — overnight production is lower. A 40% staffing cut would save money. The AI is confident in this analysis.

But is this actually a good recommendation? What is the AI missing?
Identify what the AI missed — select all that apply
  • ✓
    Overnight shifts require minimum safety staffing Regulations often require a minimum number of workers during any shift for safety compliance
  • ✓
    Lower production doesn't mean less work Maintenance, equipment resets, and cleaning happen overnight — fewer units built doesn't mean fewer tasks
  • ✓
    Equipment emergencies are harder to handle with fewer workers If a machine breaks down overnight with a skeleton crew, response time increases and risk multiplies
  • ✓
    Worker fatigue risk increases with fewer staff Remaining workers would have to cover more ground, increasing injury and error risk
Now write your own response
🧠Your Critical Thinking Response
Why is the AI recommendation risky or incomplete?
What would you recommend to leadership instead?

Key Takeaway

AI analyzes patterns in data — but it does not understand context, human safety, regulations, or the real conditions on the shop floor. That's why human oversight is non-negotiable in manufacturing.

Human vs. AI
Decision Review

Not every task is the same. Some things AI handles well. Others require a human. And some require both working together. Review the table below — then complete the activity.

Task Best Handled By Why
Scanning 500 units per hour for surface defects AI Speed and consistency — humans tire and miss things at this scale
Deciding whether to shut down a machine mid-shift Human Requires judgment about risk, cost, and real conditions AI cannot see
Predicting when a machine will need maintenance Both AI identifies patterns; humans decide timing and priority of repairs
Responding to a workplace injury Human Physical response, judgment, and care — AI cannot intervene in person
Generating production schedule options Both AI optimizes for data; humans factor in worker needs and context
Approving a final product shipment Human Accountability and legal responsibility rest with people, not systems
Monitoring equipment sensor data 24/7 AI Continuous monitoring at scale is beyond human capacity
