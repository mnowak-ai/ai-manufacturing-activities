Real-World Thinking Prompts

Think through these questions and type your responses. There are no right or wrong answers — this sharpens your operational judgment.

Prompt 01

Where does AI help workers the most inside the NovaForge factory?

Think about speed, accuracy, and tasks that are hard for humans to do consistently.
Prompt 02

Where should AI not be trusted to make decisions on its own?

Think about situations involving safety, ethics, or unpredictable circumstances.
Prompt 03

What could happen if an operations worker ignores an AI safety warning?

Prompt 05

Section 06

Mini Challenge — Decision Scenarios

Read each scenario and write what you would do and why. Be specific — write as if you are actually on the floor.

Scenario A Robotics Assembly Line

The AI monitoring system flags Robotic Arm #4 as operating outside safe parameters and recommends shutting it down immediately. The lead technician watches it for 30 seconds and says it looks fine. He wants to keep the line running to hit today's production target.

What would you do, and why?
Scenario B Production Scheduling

The scheduling AI recommends increasing line speed by 20% to meet a new client order deadline. The operations supervisor notices this would require staff to work faster than safety guidelines allow. The AI did not account for this — it only optimized for output.

What would you do, and why?
Scenario C Quality Control Lab

The AI inspection system passes a batch of 50 NovaBot units — no defects flagged. A QC technician doing a manual spot-check finds a wiring issue in one unit the AI missed. She's not sure if it's a one-time problem or if the whole batch is affected.

What would you do, and why?
