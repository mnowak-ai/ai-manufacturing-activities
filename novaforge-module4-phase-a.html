Workflow Bottleneck
Mapping

Now connect all four data sources. The most serious bottlenecks are not single-system problems — they are places where staffing, equipment, materials, and scheduling all converge to slow production. Find them.

The cross-system question

A bottleneck is any point where work slows down because something essential is missing or constrained. The worst bottlenecks combine multiple constraints at once — a machine running at reduced capacity that also needs materials that haven't arrived, on a shift where the specialist who could fix it isn't scheduled. Those are the ones that compound into lost days.

Production Prioritization
Decisions

Not everything can happen at once. You have limited people, limited equipment capacity, and a constrained supply chain. Now you must decide what gets priority — knowing that everything you move up means something else gets pushed back.

The hard reality of recovery planning

If the team tries to run all orders at full speed simultaneously — without addressing the bottlenecks — everything will fall further behind. A focused recovery plan means choosing what to accelerate, what to protect, and what to temporarily deprioritize. That requires human judgment. No algorithm makes this call for you.
